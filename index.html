DAY 01PUSH

Chest · Shoulders · Triceps

Muscles Trained
Pecs (Major & Minor) Anterior Deltoid Lateral Deltoid Triceps Serratus Anterior
Dumbbell Push Exercises
PUSH · DB · 01
Dumbbell Flat Bench Press
Primary
Pectoralis Major
Secondary
Anterior Deltoid Triceps Serratus Anterior
Lie flat, dumbbells at chest width. Press straight up then control down in a slight arc. Keep shoulder blades pinched together throughout — this protects your rotator cuff and maximises chest stretch at the bottom.
4 × 8–12 reps ▶ Watch
PUSH · DB · 02
Dumbbell Overhead Press
Primary
Anterior DeltoidLateral Deltoid
Secondary
Triceps Upper Pecs Traps
Seated or standing. Start with dumbbells at ear level, palms forward. Press overhead in a slight inward arc — avoid flaring elbows too wide. Brace your core to prevent lower-back hyperextension.
4 × 8–10 reps ▶ Watch
PUSH · DB · 03
Incline Dumbbell Press
Primary
Upper Pectoralis
Secondary
Anterior Deltoid Triceps
Set bench to 30–45°. Press at a slight inward arc. The incline shifts emphasis to the clavicular head — essential for a full upper-chest look. Keep the rep speed controlled; don't bounce at the bottom.
3 × 10–12 reps ▶ Watch
PUSH · DB · 04
Dumbbell Lateral Raise
Primary
Lateral Deltoid
Secondary
Supraspinatus Traps (upper)
Slight forward lean, slight elbow bend. Raise to shoulder height with pinkies slightly higher than thumbs (like pouring a jug). Avoid momentum — control the descent. Go light and high-rep for best stimulus.
3 × 15–20 reps ▶ Watch
PUSH · DB · 05
Dumbbell Chest Fly
Primary
Pectoralis Major (stretch)
Secondary
Anterior Deltoid Biceps (short head)
Flat bench, slight elbow bend throughout. Open arms wide in an arc until you feel a deep chest stretch, then squeeze pecs to close. Do NOT press — this is a fly. Keep the elbows at a fixed angle; the movement is pure shoulder adduction.
3 × 12–15 reps ▶ Watch
PUSH · DB · 06
Overhead Triceps Extension
Primary
Triceps (long head)
Secondary
Medial & Lateral Triceps Head
Hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead. Lower behind your head slowly by bending elbows, keeping them pointed straight up. The overhead position is key — it fully stretches the long head, which makes up 2/3 of your triceps mass.
3 × 10–14 reps ▶ Watch
Resistance Band Push Exercises
PUSH · BAND · 01
Band Chest Press
Primary
Pectoralis Major
Secondary
Anterior DeltoidTriceps
Anchor band behind you at chest height (door anchor or pole). Stand with band in each hand, press forward and slightly inward. Band resistance peaks at peak contraction — opposite to dumbbells — great for chest variety.
4 × 12–15 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BAND · 02
Band Lateral Raise
Primary
Lateral Deltoid
Secondary
SupraspinatusUpper Traps
Stand on the band, one end in each hand. Raise arms sideways to shoulder height. The band's ascending resistance adds difficulty at the top — the hardest part of the range — making this very effective for lateral head isolation.
3 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BAND · 03
Band Overhead Press
Primary
Deltoid (Anterior + Lateral)
Secondary
TricepsUpper Traps
Stand on the band, press from shoulders to overhead. Keep core braced to avoid arch. Can be done seated for more stability. Great for progressive overload when different strength bands are used.
4 × 10–12 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BAND · 04
Band Triceps Pushdown
Primary
Triceps (Lateral + Medial Head)
Secondary
Forearm Extensors
Anchor band overhead. Grip ends, keep elbows locked at sides, push down until arms are straight. Squeeze hard at the bottom. The pulldown simulates a cable machine and is one of the best triceps isolation exercises with bands.
3 × 15–18 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BAND · 05
Band Chest Fly / Cross-Body
Primary
Pectoralis Major
Secondary
Anterior Deltoid
Anchor band at shoulder height from one side. Step away and perform a single-arm cross-body fly sweeping your arm across your body. Allows excellent range of motion and constant tension — mimics cable crossovers perfectly.
3 × 12 reps each side▶ Watch
Bodyweight Push Exercises
PUSH · BW · 01
Push-Up (Chest Emphasis)
Primary
Pectoralis Major
Secondary
Anterior DeltoidTricepsSerratus Anterior
Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower chest to ground in 2–3 sec, press explosively back up. At the top, actively protract scapulae (push hands into floor). Add a weighted vest or elevate feet to increase difficulty.
4 × Max reps (or 15–25)▶ Watch
PUSH · BW · 02
Dips (Triceps/Chest)
Primary
TricepsPectoralis Major (lower)
Secondary
Anterior DeltoidRhomboids
For chest: lean forward, elbows wide. For triceps: stay upright, elbows close. Lower until upper arms are parallel to floor, press back up. This is one of the best compound upper-body exercises — weight it with a dip belt as you progress.
4 × 8–15 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BW · 03
Pike Push-Up
Primary
Anterior Deltoid
Secondary
TricepsUpper PecsTraps
Hips raised high forming an inverted V. Lower head toward floor between hands. This targets the shoulder in the same movement pattern as an overhead press. Progress to elevated pike push-ups then eventually handstand push-ups.
4 × 10–15 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BW · 04
Diamond Push-Up
Primary
Triceps
Secondary
Inner PecsAnterior Deltoid
Hands forming a diamond shape under your chest. Elbows stay close to body throughout. Targets the medial and lateral triceps heads effectively without any equipment. Keep hips level — don't let them sag or pike.
3 × 12–18 reps▶ Watch
PUSH · BW · 05
Decline Push-Up
Primary
Upper Pectoralis
Secondary
Anterior DeltoidTriceps
Feet elevated on a chair or bench, hands on floor. The decline angle shifts load to the upper chest (clavicular head), replicating an incline press. The higher the feet, the more shoulder involvement. Keep core tight throughout.
3 × 12–20 reps▶ Watch

DAY 02PULL

Back (Lats, Rhomboids, Traps) · Biceps · Rear Delts

Muscles Trained
Latissimus Dorsi Rhomboids Trapezius Rear Deltoid Biceps Brachialis Erector Spinae
Dumbbell Pull Exercises
PULL · DB · 01
Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift
Primary
HamstringsErector Spinae
Secondary
GlutesLats (stabiliser)Traps
Hold dumbbells in front of thighs. Hinge at hips pushing them back while keeping a neutral spine and soft knees. Feel a deep hamstring stretch then drive hips forward. This is a posterior chain staple — essential for pull day.
4 × 8–10 reps▶ Watch
PULL · DB · 02
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
Primary
Latissimus Dorsi
Secondary
RhomboidsRear DeltBicepsTraps
Knee and hand on bench for support. Pull dumbbell up to hip — NOT to shoulder. Elbow travels behind torso. Squeeze the lat at the top. Think "elbow to pocket" not "elbow to ceiling." One of the best lat-builders with dumbbells.
4 × 10–12 reps / side▶ Watch
PULL · DB · 03
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
Primary
RhomboidsMid-Traps
Secondary
LatsBicepsErectors
Hinge forward ~45°. Pull both dumbbells up toward your lower chest, squeezing shoulder blades together. This targets the mid-back thickness more than the single-arm row. Keep your lower back flat — no rounding.
4 × 10–12 reps▶ Watch
PULL · DB · 04
Dumbbell Bicep Curl
Primary
Biceps Brachii
Secondary
BrachialisBrachioradialis
Supinate (rotate palm up) as you curl — this is critical to engage the full biceps brachii, not just the brachialis. Avoid swinging — strict form beats heavy weight. Alternate arms or do both simultaneously. Pause at full contraction.
3 × 10–14 reps▶ Watch
PULL · DB · 05
Rear Delt Fly (Reverse Fly)
Primary
Posterior Deltoid
Secondary
RhomboidsMid-TrapsInfraspinatus
Hinge forward until torso is parallel to floor. Slight elbow bend. Raise arms outward and up in an arc, squeezing rear delts at the top. Avoid shrugging — keep traps relaxed. This is often undertrained and critical for shoulder balance and posture.
3 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
PULL · DB · 06
Hammer Curl
Primary
BrachialisBrachioradialis
Secondary
Biceps BrachiiForearm
Neutral grip (thumbs up), curl without rotation. Brachialis sits under the bicep — building it pushes the bicep up visually. Pair with supinated curls every session for complete arm development. Can be done alternating or cross-body.
3 × 12–15 reps▶ Watch
Resistance Band Pull Exercises
PULL · BAND · 01
Band Lat Pulldown
Primary
Latissimus Dorsi
Secondary
BicepsRear DeltTeres Major
Anchor band overhead. Kneel or sit below it. Pull the band down to your upper chest while slightly leaning back. Drive elbows down and back. This is the closest band equivalent to a cable lat pulldown — excellent for lats.
4 × 12–15 reps▶ Watch
PULL · BAND · 02
Band Seated Row
Primary
RhomboidsMid-Traps
Secondary
LatsBicepsRear Delt
Sit on floor, loop band around feet. Row hands to your abdomen, squeezing shoulder blades together. Maintain an upright torso — don't lean back excessively. This mimics a cable seated row perfectly.
4 × 12–15 reps▶ Watch
PULL · BAND · 03
Band Bicep Curl
Primary
Biceps Brachii
Secondary
BrachialisBrachioradialis
Stand on the band, hands supinated (palms up). Curl both hands up. The ascending resistance of the band challenges you more at peak contraction than a dumbbell does — excellent for biceps peak stimulus.
3 × 15 reps▶ Watch
PULL · BAND · 04
Band Face Pull
Primary
Rear DeltoidExternal Rotators
Secondary
Mid-TrapsRhomboidsInfraspinatus
Anchor band at face height. Pull both ends to your face with elbows flaring high and out, like a goalpost. The external rotation component at the end is critical — it trains the rotator cuff and combats internal rotation from pressing. Do this every pull session.
3 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
PULL · BAND · 05
Band Pull-Apart
Primary
Rear DeltoidMid-Traps
Secondary
RhomboidsRotator Cuff
Hold band with both hands at shoulder width in front of you. Pull apart horizontally until band touches chest. Arms stay straight. Excellent upper-back and posture exercise — often used as a warm-up or finisher. Can do 25–30 reps per set.
3 × 20–25 reps▶ Watch
Bodyweight Pull Exercises
PULL · BW · 01
Pull-Up / Chin-Up
Primary
Latissimus Dorsi
Secondary
BicepsRear DeltTeres MajorRhomboids
Pull-up (overhand) = more lats. Chin-up (underhand) = more biceps. Dead hang at bottom. Pull until chin clears bar. Avoid kipping. This is the single best bodyweight back exercise. Add weight when you can do 12+ reps.
4 × Max reps (or 5–12)▶ Watch
PULL · BW · 02
Inverted Row (Australian Pull-Up)
Primary
RhomboidsMid-Traps
Secondary
LatsBicepsRear Delt
Use a low bar or table. Lie underneath and grip the bar with straight arms. Row your chest up to the bar, squeezing shoulder blades together. Body stays straight. Easier than pull-ups — great for beginners or as supplemental work. Elevate feet to increase difficulty.
4 × 10–15 reps▶ Watch
PULL · BW · 03
Superman Hold / Back Extension
Primary
Erector Spinae
Secondary
GlutesHamstringsRhomboids
Lie face down, arms extended. Simultaneously lift arms, chest, and legs off the floor. Hold 2–3 sec at the top. Works the entire posterior chain isometrically. Slow, controlled reps are key — this is underrated as a lower-back strength builder.
3 × 12–15 reps (3 sec hold)▶ Watch
PULL · BW · 04
Doorframe Bicep Curl
Primary
Biceps Brachii
Secondary
BrachialisForearms
Grip a sturdy doorframe edge with supinated palms. Lean back and curl your bodyweight up. Adjust foot placement to control resistance. A creative no-equipment bicep solution. Can also be done with a towel looped around a pole.
3 × 12–15 reps▶ Watch

DAY 03LEGS

Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves · Hip Flexors

Muscles Trained
Quadriceps (all 4 heads) Hamstrings Gluteus Maximus Gluteus Medius Calves (Gastrocnemius + Soleus) Adductors
Dumbbell Leg Exercises
LEGS · DB · 01
Dumbbell Goblet Squat
Primary
QuadricepsGlutes
Secondary
HamstringsCoreAdductors
Hold one dumbbell vertically at chest. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Squat deep — aim to get thighs parallel or below. Elbows brush inside thighs at the bottom. The goblet position naturally keeps the torso upright, making this safer and more effective than a back squat for beginners.
4 × 10–15 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · DB · 02
Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift
Primary
HamstringsGlutes
Secondary
Erector SpinaeAdductors
Hip hinge with soft knees — NOT a back extension. Lower dumbbells along legs until deep hamstring stretch, then drive hips forward to stand. Feel the stretch; if you don't, you're squatting it. The single most important posterior-chain exercise on leg day.
4 × 8–12 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · DB · 03
Dumbbell Walking Lunge
Primary
QuadsGlutes
Secondary
HamstringsHip FlexorsCalves
Dumbbells at sides. Step forward, lower rear knee toward floor, push off front foot to take the next step. Keep front knee tracking over 2nd toe — no caving in. The unilateral nature makes this excellent for correcting left-right imbalances.
3 × 12 steps / leg▶ Watch
LEGS · DB · 04
Dumbbell Step-Up
Primary
GlutesQuads
Secondary
HamstringsCore
Hold dumbbells, step onto a sturdy bench or box with one foot. Drive through the heel of the top foot to stand on the bench. Don't push off the back foot. Control the descent. Height of box determines quad vs glute emphasis — higher box = more glutes.
3 × 10–12 reps / leg▶ Watch
LEGS · DB · 05
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
Primary
HamstringsGlutes
Secondary
Gluteus MediusCore Stabilisers
Balance on one leg, dumbbell in opposite hand. Hinge forward, extending free leg behind. Keep hips square — don't rotate open. The balance demand recruits the gluteus medius heavily. One of the best exercises for hip stability and posterior-chain development.
3 × 10 reps / leg▶ Watch
LEGS · DB · 06
Standing Calf Raise
Primary
Gastrocnemius
Secondary
SoleusTibialis Posterior
Hold dumbbells, stand on edge of a step for full range of motion. Lower heel all the way down for a deep stretch, then rise to full tippy-toe. The full range of motion (especially the bottom stretch) is key — partial reps leave gains on the table. Calves respond well to high reps.
4 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
Resistance Band Leg Exercises
LEGS · BAND · 01
Band Squat
Primary
QuadricepsGlutes
Secondary
HamstringsCore
Stand on band, hold ends at shoulders (or crossed over chest). Squat as deep as mobility allows. Bands apply more resistance at the top — this actually reinforces the lock-out and glute activation that bodyweight squats often miss. Can also loop around thighs for a wider activation.
4 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · BAND · 02
Band Hip Thrust / Glute Bridge
Primary
Gluteus Maximus
Secondary
HamstringsCoreHip Flexors (stretch)
Place band over hips, anchored under hands or feet. Lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up by squeezing glutes hard. Hold 1–2 sec at the top — this squeeze is everything. Glute bridges are the most direct glute stimulus available without a barbell. Don't hyperextend your lower back.
4 × 15–20 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · BAND · 03
Band Monster Walk / Lateral Walk
Primary
Gluteus Medius
Secondary
Gluteus MinimusTFLHip Abductors
Loop mini-band around ankles or just above knees. Quarter-squat position. Step sideways, keeping tension in the band at all times. Walk 10–15 steps each direction. This is the single best exercise for gluteus medius — critical for knee health and athletic power.
3 × 15 steps / direction▶ Watch
LEGS · BAND · 04
Band Hamstring Curl (Prone)
Primary
Hamstrings
Secondary
Gastrocnemius
Anchor band at ground level, loop around ankles. Lie face down. Curl both heels toward glutes against band resistance. This is one of the few ways to isolate hamstrings without a machine. Squeeze at full contraction and control the negative.
3 × 15 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · BAND · 05
Band Clamshell
Primary
Gluteus Medius
Secondary
Gluteus MinimusPiriformis
Lie on your side, knees bent to 90°, band above knees. Rotate top knee open like a clamshell — don't let your hips roll back. Isolates the hip abductors and external rotators. Essential for knee stability, especially if you have valgus knee collapse (knees caving in on squats).
3 × 20 reps / side▶ Watch
Bodyweight Leg Exercises
LEGS · BW · 01
Bulgarian Split Squat
Primary
QuadsGlutes
Secondary
HamstringsHip FlexorsCore
Rear foot elevated on a bench, front foot out in front. Lower your body straight down until rear knee is just off the floor. Keep torso upright (quad emphasis) or lean forward (glute emphasis). This is arguably harder than a barbell squat — you will feel this in one exercise alone.
4 × 8–12 reps / leg▶ Watch
LEGS · BW · 02
Jump Squat
Primary
QuadsGlutes
Secondary
HamstringsCalvesCore
Squat down then explode upward, leaving the ground. Land softly on mid-foot, immediately absorb into the next squat. Develops explosive power and fast-twitch muscle fibers. Adds metabolic and cardiovascular stimulus to a leg session. Do NOT do these if knees are sore.
3 × 10–15 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · BW · 03
Glute Bridge (Single-Leg)
Primary
Gluteus Maximus
Secondary
HamstringsCore
Lie on back, one knee bent foot flat, other leg extended. Drive through the grounded heel to raise hips until body forms a straight line. Squeeze glute hard at the top. The single-leg version doubles the load and demands core stability. Progress to elevated single-leg hip thrust.
3 × 15 reps / leg▶ Watch
LEGS · BW · 04
Wall Sit
Primary
Quadriceps (isometric)
Secondary
GlutesHip Flexors
Back flat against wall, thighs parallel to floor, knees at 90°. Hold the position. This is a pure quad isometric hold — brutal and effective. Time yourself and try to beat your previous record. Can be loaded by placing a weight plate on your thighs. Great finisher at end of leg day.
3 × 45–90 sec hold▶ Watch
LEGS · BW · 05
Nordic Hamstring Curl
Primary
Hamstrings (eccentric)
Secondary
GlutesCore
Kneel on a mat, feet anchored under a couch/partner. Lower your body to the floor as slowly as possible by extending at the knees — use hands to push back up if needed. One of the most scientifically validated injury-prevention exercises for hamstrings. Start with just 3–5 reps; it's extremely demanding.
3 × 5–8 reps▶ Watch
LEGS · BW · 06
Standing Calf Raise (BW)
Primary
Gastrocnemius
Secondary
Soleus
On a step edge, lower heels maximally then rise to full toe. Do these slowly — 3 sec up, 3 sec down. For added challenge, do them single-leg. The deep heel drop at the bottom is non-negotiable for full gastrocnemius stretch. 100+ reps per session is not unreasonable for calf growth.
4 × 25–30 reps▶ Watch
